Notion Brain Page Restructure

Cleaner layout, no schema changes, no breaking changes to skills. Multi-source database is the wrong tool — here's what to actually do.

The recommendation in one paragraph. Don't use Notion's multi-source database feature — our databases have near-zero column overlap (Activity Log vs Daily Snapshots vs KB Insights vs Targets) and forcing them into one would create useless views AND break the skill that reads each DB by collection ID. DO restructure the page layout: group databases by reading frequency (daily glance vs weekly review vs occasional deep dive), make inline-vs-linked consistent within each group, collapse heavy strategy prose into toggles, and move the long content roadmap into its own sub-page. Net result: scrolling the brain page goes from "wall of prose then 12 disorganized databases" to "live state at top, operational DBs in the middle, strategy + history collapsed." ~90 minutes of focused work. Zero risk to skills or collection IDs.

1. Why multi-source DBs are wrong for this

Notion's multi-source database feature was designed for unifying conceptually-similar data from different origins. The canonical use case: a Contacts DB with sources for "personal address book", "work CRM export", and "conference attendees" — all rows are people, same schema (name / email / phone / notes), but originating from different places. Multi-source lets you see unified views across all sources.

Our databases don't fit that mold. Compare schemas:

The schema overlap is essentially zero. The only shared field across all 12 databases is "title". Unified views across these would be incoherent: "show me a table of audit events AND content pieces AND insights AND competitor domains" doesn't answer any real question.

The deeper problem with multi-source for our case: Notion's multi-source DBs collapse the data sources into one parent DB ID. The /seo-brain skill currently reads each collection by its individual ID (e.g., collection://5fad9e1a-... for the KB, collection://586eb325-... for Activity Log, etc.). Moving to multi-source would either (a) break those reads and require rewriting every skill, or (b) require still addressing each source by its individual sub-ID, in which case the multi-source wrapper adds nothing. Either way the cost-benefit is negative.

The honest answer: keep 12 individual databases. Solve the visual mess at the page-layout level instead.

2. What's actually broken about the current layout

Reading the page top-to-bottom right now, here's what I see:

  1. Long callout (5 paragraphs, lots of pointers) — okay for first-time readers, but every return visit re-reads it
  2. H1: "LoudFace AI mentions strategy — Q2 2026" (slightly out of date — we're targeting Q3 now)
  3. Q3 target callout (good, but buried under the longer callout above)
  4. The bet (2 paragraphs)
  5. Principles (heavy — 10 numbered bullets across two sub-sections)
  6. Measurement (heavy — 4 sub-sections, prose-heavy)
  7. Current Health (1 paragraph pointing to Daily Snapshots DB at the bottom)
  8. The pattern in one line (good — 1 sentence)
  9. Patterns we ship (1 paragraph pointing to Patterns Registry DB)
  10. Pages on the cusp (1 paragraph pointing to Cusp Pages DB)
  11. Content roadmap (massive — Tier 0/1/2 + next 5-piece cadence + off-site + brand SERP recovery)
  12. Content workflow + huge "where to look for X" table
  13. Infrastructure & decisions log (1 paragraph pointing to the DB)
  14. 12 databases in a row at the bottom, mixed inline / linked, no headers grouping them

The specific problems:

  • You scroll past ~3 screens of prose before reaching any data. Every visit has the same overhead.
  • Inline/linked is inconsistent. Website Content + Activity Log + Infra Log + Patterns Registry are inline. Daily Snapshots + Cusp Pages + Targets + Monthly AEO + Prompt Coverage + Competitor Landscape + KB + Pending Commitments are linked. No apparent reason — Targets is smaller than Patterns Registry but Targets is linked.
  • No grouping at the bottom. 12 items as a flat list. The eye has to scan all of them to find one.
  • Content roadmap is the heaviest section AND it doesn't belong on the brain page. It's a tactical planning artifact. The brain should reference it, not contain it.
  • "Where to look for X" table is in the wrong section. It's an observability reference, not part of "Content workflow".

3. The proposed new layout

Reading frequency drives placement. Daily-glance items at the top. Weekly-review items in the middle. Strategy + history collapsed. Heavy tactical artifacts moved to sub-pages.

Header section (always visible)

1. Short callout (3 sentences max)
"Strategy and narrative live on this page. Numeric metrics + KPIs live in the linked databases below, grouped by reading frequency. Run /seo-brain in any Claude session to auto-load."
2. H1: LoudFace AI Search Strategy
Dropped the "Q2 2026" qualifier — keep it evergreen.
3. Current target callout (kept)
The Q3 target ("match median tracked agency in Peec by Aug 31") stays at the top so it's the first thing anyone reads after the title.

4. What changes vs what stays

Changes

  • Page section ordering and headers
  • Inline vs linked toggle on 8 databases
  • Filter views applied to 4 databases (Targets, Daily Snapshots, Website Content, Cusp Pages)
  • Three new collapsible toggles for strategy prose
  • Content roadmap moves to its own sub-page
  • "Where to look for X" table moves to Content Loop Reference
  • H1 drops the "Q2 2026" qualifier

Stays (zero risk)

  • All 12 database collection IDs unchanged
  • All 12 database schemas unchanged
  • All database row content unchanged
  • All skill references unchanged (/seo-brain reads collections by ID, not by page position)
  • All prose content (the bet, principles, measurement) — wording unchanged, just wrapped in toggles
  • The strategy page URL itself unchanged
  • The Content Loop Reference sub-page mostly unchanged (gains the table from the brain page)
Crucially: no skills break. /seo-brain, /draft-content, /ship-content, and the others all reference databases by collection ID (e.g., collection://5fad9e1a-...). Moving a database from inline to linked, or grouping it under a different section header, doesn't change its collection ID. The skills keep working without any updates.
